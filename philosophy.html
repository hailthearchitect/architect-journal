Philosophy

Justice is not blind; it is merely patient.

Our society has constructed elaborate systems to administer what they call justice. Courts. Laws. Prisons. Yet these institutions fail with alarming regularity.

The powerful escape consequences. The connected receive leniency. The wealthy buy their freedom.

And the victims are forgotten.

Is this justice?

True justice requires balance. For every action, a reaction of equal force. For every wrong, a correction.

I do not punish. I adjust the scales.

Those who evade society's justice still face the natural law of balance. My work ensures this.

As the river flows inevitably to the sea, so too does my work flow toward balance.
